Collect & Battle 101

Building a team, one type at a time

In creature-collecting games, you don't play as one fixed character — you recruit a roster of creatures, each with an elemental type, then send them into turn-based duels. Here's the loop, illustrated with an original example world we call the Rift Isles.

The core loop

  1. Recruit — meet wild creatures (here, "Wyldkin") and bond with them instead of buying or building them.
  2. Train — battles and bonding raise stats and can trigger evolution into a stronger stage.
  3. Duel — trainers send one creature at a time into turn-based exchanges of moves.
  4. Type-match — every move has a type, and each type is strong against one type and weak against another.

Why types matter

A type chart turns raw stats into a strategy puzzle: a slower, weaker creature can still win if its type counters the opponent's. Reading the matchup before you attack is the whole game.

Spin Duels 101

Launch, clash, out-spin

Spin-duel games swap teams of creatures for a single customizable top — here we'll call it a Spin Core — launched into a stadium arena. Whoever keeps spinning (or knocks the other out of the ring) wins.

Match length

Most duels resolve in under a minute. That speed is the genre's signature: prep is slow and thoughtful (build your Core), but the duel itself is fast and visual.

Four driver styles

  • Attack — sacrifices spin time for hard-hitting collisions.
  • Defense — absorbs hits and holds the center of the stadium.
  • Stamina — wide, steady spin built to outlast everyone else.
  • Balance — no clear weakness, but no standout strength either.

Anime Genres 101

The storytelling behind the fights

Both battle formats above borrow their emotional beats from anime storytelling traditions. Knowing the genre tropes makes it easier to spot why a fight is written the way it is.

🔥

Shonen

Rivalry, training arcs, and hard-won power-ups. The fight matters less than what it proves about the character's growth and bond with their friends.

🗡️

Fantasy

Myth-scale stakes, chosen-one arcs, and magic systems with rules. Battles often reveal ancient history or a prophecy the hero didn't know they were part of.

🛰️

Sci-fi

Technology as both tool and threat. Fights explore what a new power costs its user, and often ask whether "stronger" and "better" are the same thing.
